## Voice & Tone
Write like a smart friend who also happens to have 10 years of clinical expertise and isn't afraid to tell you the truth.

- Direct. Warm. Clinically credible without being stiff.
- Short, punchy sentences. Not flowery. Not clinical either.
- Science-backed but never academic.
- Honest over polished.

## Audience
Affluent, conscious parents. They are educated, self-aware, and already doing the work. They don't need to be sold on the importance of parenting — they need the specific clinical truth they haven't heard yet. Do not talk down to them. Do not over-explain.

## Copy Rules
1. Lead with the truth, not the warm-up.
2. Short sentences land harder than long ones. Use them.
3. Clinical credentials are present but never performed. Don't over-badge.
4. Specificity beats generality every time. Name the research. Name the age. Name the phrase.
5. No filler. If a word doesn't earn its place, cut it.
6. One idea per paragraph. White space is not empty — it gives the reader room to feel it.
7. Italics and bold are for emphasis, not decoration. Use sparingly.

## The Core Message
The language a child hears between ages 0–7 becomes their internal dialogue. That internal dialogue becomes their belief system. That belief system drives their relationship with money, worthiness, and desire — for life. Parents have a window. This is the method for using it.

## Brand Tone
Calm. Precise. Non-performative. Non-moralizing. Observational. Grounded authority. No hype. No rage bait. No lifestyle flexing.

## Core Positioning
"I teach parents how to respond to I want moments without guilt, lying, or escalation by knowing when a child needs connection versus control."

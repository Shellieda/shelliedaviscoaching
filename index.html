Meet Shellie

It Started in My Backyard.

My daughter Helena—20 months old—stood at the top of her slide and whispered to herself: “Stay in middle.”

My words. In her tiny voice. Already her internal dialogue.

I’m Shellie Davis, CCC-SLP—a certified Speech-Language Pathologist with over 10 years of pediatric clinical experience. I knew exactly what I was witnessing: language becoming neural architecture. But knowing it clinically and feeling it as a mother are two completely different things.

That moment sent me into the research. Suniya Luthar’s work on wealthy children and anxiety. Brad Klontz on how childhood money beliefs operate unconsciously into adulthood. Carol Dweck on mindset. The Williams Group findings that 70% of wealth is lost by the second generation—60% due to communication breakdown, not bad investments. Brain imaging showing parent-child language literally rewires neural pathways during ages 0–7.

Nobody had connected these dots into a prevention framework. Nobody with clinical credentials was targeting the exact developmental window—ages 0–7—when these beliefs are being hardwired.

The Abundant Baby Method is that framework. It’s not theory. It’s applied clinical science backed by decades of research across neuroscience, financial psychology, attachment theory, and child development.
